## Unit Overview for Teachers

**Tier 3 unit — 3–4 weeks.**

Space is the most misconception-dense unit in Grade 6 Science relative to its length. Three specific errors appear almost universally: diagrams show the solar system to scale (they don't), planets produce their own light (they reflect it), and Pluto's reclassification was arbitrary or unfair. Each of these needs direct address before students will release the misconception.

**The Pluto discussion is a gift.** It is the clearest real-world example in the entire course of science being self-correcting: new evidence → updated classification. Use it every time a student says "scientists just make it up" or "they keep changing their minds."

## CS Integration

Two CS connections in this unit. Lesson 5: Ex-Alta 1 is a computational artifact — sensor in a hard-to-reach place, connected to a human-readable output through a data pipeline. Lesson 4: computer models can simulate conditions no physical tool can reach (inside a star, billions of years into the past), but are only as reliable as the data and assumptions that built them.

## Misconception Map

| Misconception | When it appears | How to address |
|---|---|---|
| "Textbook diagrams show the solar system to scale" | Lesson 3 | Basketball/pinhead demonstration — 30 m to nearest planet |
| "Planets produce their own light like stars" | Lesson 2 | Define emit vs. reflect; Venus at dusk is reflected sunlight |
| "Pluto's reclassification was arbitrary/unfair" | Lesson 8 | Three conditions; Kuiper Belt objects; science updates when evidence demands it |
| "Satellites need constant thrust to stay in orbit" | Lesson 6 | Orbit = falling forward fast enough that Earth curves away beneath |
