## Unit Overview for Teachers

**Tier 2 unit — 3–4 weeks.**

Energy Resources is the most values-laden unit in Grade 6 Science. Students will have strong opinions about fossil fuels and renewable energy. The teacher's job is to ground those opinions in evidence: what do the five selection factors actually tell us about each resource? There is no correct answer in the Alberta town scenario — there are only trade-offs with evidence.

**Key discipline:** keep students in the evidence. When they say "we should just use renewables," ask: "Which selection factors support that? Which ones create challenges?" When they say "oil is bad," ask: "What do the five factors tell us?"

## CS Integration

Computational artifacts create unintended consequences. Lesson 5: smart grids, electric vehicles, and renewable infrastructure are designed systems that solve emissions problems but create new trade-offs — rare earth mining, e-waste, grid instability. Technology design is not neutral. Every solution creates new problems to monitor.

## Misconception Map

| Misconception | When it appears | How to address |
|---|---|---|
| "Renewable = clean / no environmental impact" | Lesson 1 | Hydroelectric dams flood ecosystems; wind turbines affect birds; solar panel manufacturing uses toxic materials |
| "Before processing = renewable, after processing = non-renewable" | Lesson 3 | Wind (renewable) is used both before and after processing; the categories are independent |
| "We should always choose the cheapest option" | Lesson 6 | Five-factor analysis: cheap now may cost more long-term (environmental damage, responsible management) |
