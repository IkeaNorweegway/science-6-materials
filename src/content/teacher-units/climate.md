## Unit Overview for Teachers

**Tier 2 unit — 4–5 weeks.**

Climate is the unit where evidence quality becomes the central skill. Students must learn to distinguish weather from climate, evaluate the timescale of a claim, and understand what counts as long-term data. The greenhouse effect vs. ozone hole misconception is the highest-stakes error — confusing them leads to wrong conclusions about both cause and solution.

**Key discipline:** students need to understand that science operates on timescales, not single events. Every time a student says "it was cold last winter, so climate change is wrong," that is a teachable moment about signal vs. noise.

## CS Integration

Climate models are computational artifacts — programs that simulate Earth's climate system. Lesson 10 explores: what is the model keeping? What is it leaving out? Why is this an abstraction? Connect to force diagrams (Forces unit) and the particle model (Matter unit) as prior examples of abstraction in science.

## Misconception Map

| Misconception | When it appears | How to address |
|---|---|---|
| "Weather and climate are the same thing" | Lesson 1 | Timescale chart — weather = days, climate = decades |
| "One cold event disproves climate change" | Lesson 1, throughout | Signal vs. noise diagram — one data point does not define a trend |
| "Greenhouse effect = ozone hole" | Lesson 2, Lesson 5 | Dedicated callout — different gases, different layers, different effects |
| "Climate change is a future problem" | Lesson 6 | Alberta data: 1.5°C rise already measured, wildfire seasons already longer |
| "Natural variation means humans aren't responsible" | Lesson 5 | Rate of change argument: 150 years vs. tens of thousands for natural cycles |
