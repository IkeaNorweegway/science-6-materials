EF workbooks use word banks. For fill-in questions, accept any answer from the word bank that correctly fills the blank. For open response, accept any response that includes the key idea noted.

Q#Concept / TypeAnswer / Marking Guide
Q1 Matter Particle model — 4 ideas · fill-in list 1. Particles make up all matter. 2. Particles are always moving. 3. Particles have empty space between them. 4. Different particles have different properties.
Q2 Matter Phase changes · label diagram A = melting (solid→liquid) · B = evaporation (liquid→gas) · C = freezing (liquid→solid) · D = condensation (gas→liquid) · E = sublimation (solid→gas, skips liquid)
Q3 Matter Ice melts — mass conservation · word bank + explain Mass of water: 75 g. Because: conservation of mass — particles do not disappear when matter changes state. Only the arrangement of particles changes; no particles are added or removed.
Q4 Matter Ice floats — particle model · fill-in In ice, particles are more spread out than in liquid water. This makes ice less dense (lower density). Less dense things floats on denser liquids.
Q5 Matter Freeze-thaw weathering · word bank chain When water freezes it expands (takes up more space). The ice pushes against the rock. The crack widens gets bigger over time. This process is called freeze-thaw weathering. (Particle model link: particles in ice are more spread out, so water expands as it freezes.)

Q9 Forces Newton's Third Law + examples · word bank + fill-in Newton's Third Law: for every action there is an equal and opposite reaction — forces act on different objects. Example 1 (any): jumping — you push floor down; floor pushes you up. Example 2 (any): rowing — paddle pushes water back; water pushes boat forward. OR rocket — gases push down; rocket is pushed up.
Q10 Forces Elastic limit — paper clip · fill-in Small bend: force was below the elastic limit → elastic deformation → paper clip returns to shape. Large bend: force was above the elastic limit → plastic deformation → paper clip keeps its bent shape.

Q13 Living Systems Evaluate two student statements · word bank Student 1 (plants get food from soil) = Incorrect. Q16 Living Systems Invasive insect cascade (aspen) · chain fill-in Invasive insect → aspen trees die → woodpeckers lose nesting habitat and food → their population decreases · moose lose food source → their population decreases → food web disruption / habitat loss cascades further. Award marks for naming two species with logical effects.

Unit 4 — Climate (Q17–Q21)

Q#Concept / TypeAnswer / Marking Guide
Q17 Climate Greenhouse effect — four steps · fill-in list 1. sun's energy enters the atmosphere. 2. It heats Earth's surface. 3. greenhouse gases trap the heat that the surface gives off. 4. Heat is re-radiated back toward Earth. This warms Earth.
Q18 Climate Cold March misconception · word bank + explain The error is: using one event (weather) to disprove a long-term trend (climate). One cold month does not prove or disprove climate change. Climate change is shown by: decades of data — a long-term trend across many years, not a single event.
Q19 Climate Temperature trend table · word bank + describe The trend: temperatures are rising / warming trend — values go from 0.0°C to +1.1°C above baseline. Warming is accelerating (the increase is getting faster over time). This is above baseline and increasing.
Q20 Climate Albedo feedback loop · chain fill-in Temperatures rise → ice melts → less ice → dark ocean exposed → dark ocean absorbs more heat → temperatures rise more. This is a positive feedback loop (warming accelerates itself).
Q21 Climate TEK adds to scientific climate data · word bank + explain TEK adds to science because: it provides long-term observations spanning generations — from before instruments existed. It carries local knowledge of specific places and changes. TEK can fill gaps in scientific data and confirms scientific data from earlier periods.

Q27 Space Eight planets in order · word bank fill-in 1. Mercury · 2. Venus · 3. Earth · 4. Mars · 5. Jupiter · 6. Saturn · 7. Uranus · 8. Neptune. (Mnemonic: My Very Eager Mother Just Served Us Nachos.)
Q28 Space Natural vs artificial satellite · word bank + examples Natural satellite: formed naturally, orbits another body. Example: Earth's Moon. Artificial satellite: made by humans, launched into orbit. Example: ISS OR GPS satellite.
Q29 Space Moon glows? · misconception + word bank The student is not correct. We see the Moon because it reflects sunlight. It does not produce light of its own. The Moon is not a star. Only stars emit their own light.
Q30 Space Pluto reclassification · word bank + explain Pluto was reclassified because: it did not clear its orbit — it shares the Kuiper Belt with many similar objects. Scientists applied new criteria. This was the right decision because: science is evidence-based — when new evidence and better definitions emerge, science updates. That is a strength, not a flaw.
Q31 Space Ex-Alta 1 — two reasons it mattered · word bank (choose 2) Any two from: students contributed real science (not a simulation) · collected real data on space weather · showed that young people can do science · the satellite was launched into orbit and performed real measurements. Accept any two WB answers with a short explanation of why each is meaningful.
Q32 Space Indigenous astronomical knowledge + modern science · word bank + explain The connection: Indigenous peoples made long-term observations of star patterns, seasonal cycles, and navigation across generations — this is traditional knowledge that asks the same fundamental questions as modern astronomy. Indigenous knowledge complements modern astronomy by adding historical depth, local context, and observations from before written records. Both systems study patterns in the sky.

Stretch Zone — Cross-Unit Challenge (S1–S6)

Q#Concept / TypeAnswer / Marking Guide
S1 Matter + Forces: cable under tension · open response Particle model: tension pulls particles farther apart → spacing between particles increases → the cable gets longer. If force exceeds the elastic limit: plastic deformation occurs — particles are displaced permanently, the cable does not return to its original length and may eventually snap. Award marks for: particle-level explanation of elongation AND correct use of elastic limit to explain failure.
S2 Living Systems + Climate: wetland peat feedback · draw + explain Feedback loop: climate change → temperatures rise → wetland dries out → peat decomposes → bacteria release CO₂ → more CO₂ in atmosphere → more warming → more wetlands dry. This is a positive feedback loop — the effect (warming) amplifies the cause (warming). Award marks for: four linked steps in the loop AND identifying it as a positive feedback.
S3 Energy + Climate: Alberta oil economy vs climate crisis · open response Pro-oil: Alberta's economy, tax revenues, and many jobs depend on the oil industry; an abrupt phase-out would cause significant economic hardship and instability. Pro-action: climate change threatens Alberta's water supply, agriculture, and long-term economic viability; delayed action increases future costs and risks. Accept any position stated with one argument on each side. No single right answer.
S4 Space + Energy: solar satellites · open response using selection factors Using three selection factors: Availability — 24-hour sunlight in orbit (no weather or day/night cycle) → highly reliable. Environmental impact — no CO₂ during operation, but launch creates emissions. Cost — extremely expensive to build and launch; microwave transmission infrastructure on Earth required. Efficiency — very high in orbit (no atmosphere filtering). Reliability — technically reliable but vulnerable to microwave transmission losses and orbital debris. Award marks for applying at least three named factors with correct reasoning.
S5 All units — TEK across two units · open response Example response (Matter + Climate): Indigenous knowledge of ice — reading ice thickness, colour, and texture to assess safety — connects directly to particle model (density, particle spacing). In climate: Indigenous river observations (ice break-up timing, water levels) complement instrumental climate records by providing long-term data from before instruments existed. Accept any two well-explained unit connections using TEK. Award marks for naming a specific TEK contribution in each unit and explaining how it connects to the science.
S6 All units — one most important idea · open response Open response. Accept any well-reasoned choice. Award marks for: (1) identifying one idea, (2) explaining why it is important, (3) connecting it to at least two units. Example: "Everything is connected — removing one species can change a river (Living Systems + Climate + Forces); burning fossil fuels affects ecosystems, climate, and even space weather monitoring." No single right answer.
