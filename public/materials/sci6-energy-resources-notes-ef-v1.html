Section 1 — Renewable and Non-Renewable Energy

We need energy for everything. Heating our homes. Cooking food. Running cars. Charging phones.

Energy comes from resources. Some resources run out. Some do not.

Non-renewable energy

Non-renewable means it will run out. We cannot make more of it quickly.

  • Coal, oil, and natural gas — these took millions of years to form
  • Once we use them up, they are gone
  • Burning them releases carbon dioxide — a greenhouse gas

Renewable energy

Renewable means it will not run out. It is replaced naturally.

  • Solar — energy from the Sun
  • Wind — energy from moving air
  • Water (hydroelectric) — energy from moving water
  • Biomass — energy from plants and organic matter

💡 Remember
Non-renewable = limited supply. Renewable = keeps coming back. No energy source is perfect.

Section 2 — Before and After Processing

Some energy resources are used directly. Others are processed first — turned into electricity.

This is a different way of sorting energy. It is NOT the same as renewable vs. non-renewable.

Used directly (before processing)

  • Burning wood to heat a home — used directly
  • Wind turning a sail on a boat — used directly
  • Water turning a mill wheel — used directly

Turned into electricity (after processing)

  • Coal burned to generate electricity — processed
  • Wind turbines making electricity — processed
  • Solar panels making electricity — processed

Notice: wind can be used BOTH ways. Directly (sailboat) and processed (wind turbine). This surprises many people.

Section 3 — How Do We Choose an Energy Resource?

No energy source is perfect. Choosing the right one means asking five questions.

The five selection factors

FactorQuestion to ask
Availability Is this resource available here? Is there enough of it?
Societal impact How does it affect people's health and communities?
Economic impact How much does it cost to build and run? Does it create jobs?
Environmental impact Does it pollute? Does it harm animals or land?
Responsible management Are we using it carefully? Are we thinking about the future?

In Alberta, oil and gas are available and create many jobs. But they also release greenhouse gases. These trade-offs are real. Decision-makers have to weigh all five factors.

Five factors: Availability · Societal · Economic · Environmental · Responsible management. Every energy choice involves trade-offs between these.
