Stretch Zone — Challenge Questions
S1
If you shrank the solar system so that the Sun was a basketball, how large and how far away would Earth be? Use the scale factor (Sun = 22 cm diameter, real Sun = 1.4 million km) to estimate. Show your thinking.
S2
The Voyager 1 spacecraft was launched in 1977. It is now in interstellar space — beyond the solar system. It is about 23 billion km from Earth. Calculate how many AU this is (1 AU = 150 million km). What does this tell you about the scale of space beyond our solar system?
S3
Scientists recently discovered an exoplanet (a planet outside our solar system) in the "habitable zone" of its star — meaning liquid water might exist there. The planet is 40 light-years away. Even at the speed of light, it would take 40 years to get a signal there. What challenges does this create for exploring or communicating with other star systems?
S4
Many First Nations groups in Canada have traditional knowledge of specific stars and constellations. This knowledge guided travel, predicted seasons, and marked ceremonies. How could collaboration between Indigenous knowledge holders and modern astronomers benefit both groups?
